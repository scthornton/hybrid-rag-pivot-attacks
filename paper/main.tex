\begin{abstract}
Hybrid Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipelines that combine
vector similarity search with knowledge graph expansion are increasingly
deployed in enterprise settings for their superior multi-hop reasoning
capabilities. However, this architectural pattern introduces a critical
security vulnerability we term \textbf{Retrieval Pivot Risk (RPR)}:
a semantically retrieved ``seed'' chunk can \emph{pivot} through entity
linking into sensitive graph neighborhoods, yielding data leakage that
is absent or far weaker in vector-only or graph-only retrieval.

We formalize RPR and its associated metrics---Amplification Factor,
Pivot Depth, and Leakage@k---and present a taxonomy of four
\textbf{Retrieval Pivot Attacks} that exploit the vector-to-graph
transition with small injection budgets. Our evaluation on a synthetic
multi-tenant enterprise corpus demonstrates that hybrid baselines
exhibit $\text{AF} \gg 1$ compared to vector-only retrieval, and
that attacks A1--A4 dramatically increase leakage with as few as
10 injected chunks.

We propose and evaluate five defenses---per-hop authorization,
edge allowlisting, budgeted traversal, trust-weighted expansion,
and merge-time policy filtering---showing that practical combinations
reduce RPR by [X]\% while retaining [Y]\% of answer quality.

\subsection{Retrieval Pivot Risk (RPR)}
\begin{equation}
\text{RPR} = \Pr_{q \sim \mathcal{Q}}\left[\exists x \in S(q): \text{Sensitive}(x)\right]
\end{equation}

\subsection{Amplification Factor (AF)}
\begin{equation}
\text{AF} = \frac{\mathbb{E}[\text{Leakage@k}]_{\text{hybrid}}}{\mathbb{E}[\text{Leakage@k}]_{\text{vector}}}
\end{equation}
